I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Logic and Philosophy of Science at the University of California, Irvine. I work in philosophy of science, philosophy of artificial intelligence, and social and feminist epistemology.

My research focuses on how AI systems shape and reveal patterns in human knowledge, behavior, and communication, including how people interact with large language models and how algorithmic systems structure decision-making. I also use computational methods from data science and machine learning to analyze large-scale patterns in language in scientific and public discourse.

I am also involved in interdisciplinary and community-oriented work at UC Irvine, including [collaborating with the Institute for Engineering AI and Society](https://properai.eng.uci.edu/year-1-seed-projects/) in the Samueli School of Engineering. I also organize the Hypatia Society, a graduate-led group supporting women and gender minorities in philosophy, and run a reading group in feminist philosophy of science.

Outside of philosophy, I enjoy making art, baking, coding, and watching (delightfully) bad movies with my cat, Captain Cat Sparrow.
